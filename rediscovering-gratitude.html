Studies that Can Help You Rediscover Gratitude

Discover recent studies that revealed useful insights on best gratitude practices and how to apply those practices in our own lives.

While the broader society primarily focuses on gratitude during National Gratitude Month in November, a dedicated group of researchers tirelessly explores the concept of gratitude throughout the year. Their work delves deep into understanding what gratitude truly means, its numerous benefits, and practical ways to cultivate it in our daily lives.

Emerging Studies on Gratitude

Greater Good has been at the forefront of reporting on groundbreaking research that sheds light on gratitude's role in various aspects of life, from mitigating workplace stress to enhancing team dynamics and serving as a psychological anchor during the tumultuous times of the COVID-19 pandemic.

A Closer Look at Recent Findings

2022 brought forward several intriguing studies on gratitude, each offering valuable lessons on the most effective ways to practice gratitude, understanding cultural differences in expressing thanks, and finding the motivation to start personal gratitude practices. Here's what researchers discovered:

  • The Impact of Gratitude Letters vs. Journals

    Researchers at the University of California, Riverside embarked on a mission to identify the most beneficial gratitude practice for enhancing happiness. Involving 958 Australian adults in an experiment that compared different gratitude practices, they discovered that writing a gratitude letter, even without sending it, led to significant increases in gratitude, positive emotions, elevation, and a sense of connectedness. Interestingly, this method also invoked a stronger feeling of indebtedness compared to other practices.

  • The Search for the "Best" Gratitude Practice

    In a subsequent study by the same team, the focus shifted to comparing gratitude letters with practices that involved direct expression of gratitude to others. Over 900 undergraduate students participated, exploring the effects of writing and sending gratitude letters, sending thank-you texts, and expressing gratitude on social media. Surprisingly, all methods yielded similar benefits in terms of enhanced positive emotions and life satisfaction. However, sending a thank-you text slightly edged out other methods in making participants feel more connected and supported.

  • Understanding Gratitude from Childhood

    A fascinating study examined when children begin to recognize and value expressions of gratitude. Observing 40 preschoolers, researchers found that even at the tender age of four, children can discern grateful from merely happy responses to gifts. This early appreciation for gratitude suggests its foundational role in forming trust and navigating social relationships from a young age.

  • Gratitude Apps and Mental Health During the Pandemic

    The COVID-19 pandemic tested our collective resilience, leading to increased levels of stress and isolation. A study involving European adults highlighted the efficacy of using a gratitude app to combat these challenges. Participants who used the app reported significant improvements in mental health, attributed to reduced rumination and an increased ability to reframe negative experiences positively.

  • Shielding Ourselves from Regret Through Gratitude

    Another study revealed gratitude's potential in mitigating feelings of regret. Participants who engaged in gratitude reflections were found to dwell less on past mistakes, suggesting gratitude's powerful role in keeping us anchored in the present and protecting us from the paralyzing effects of regret.

  • Navigating Cultural Nuances in Expressing Gratitude

    Cultural differences play a crucial role in how gratitude is expressed and received. Research comparing Western and South Korean practices around asking for favors revealed differing preferences for apologies and thank-yous. A study in Japan further complicated these findings, showing that a simple "thank you" could be more effective than apologies in certain contexts, underscoring the importance of considering cultural backgrounds in gratitude expressions.

Broadening Our Understanding of Gratitude

As we continue to navigate life's ups and downs, the growing body of research on gratitude offers a beacon of hope. It not only highlights the multifaceted nature of gratitude but also affirms its significant impact on our mental health and social relationships. By embracing gratitude in its various forms and understanding its cultural nuances, we can enhance our well-being and foster deeper connections with those around us.

Citation:

Newman, K. M., Villareal H. J., Suttie, J., Abdullah, M. (2022, November 22). Six New Studies That Can Help You Rediscover Gratitude.
