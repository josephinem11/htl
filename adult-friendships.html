The Intricate Link Between Adult Friendship and Well-Being

Explore how significant of an impact that adult friendships have on wellbeing.

While gratitude gains widespread attention in November, National Gratitude Month, a cadre of researchers dedicates their careers to exploring the nuances of gratitude, particularly within the context of adult friendships and their impact on wellbeing. Drawing from the multifaceted PERMA theory, their review scrutinizes 38 studies from 2000 to 2019, unveiling how adult friendships serve as a cornerstone for wellbeing.

Deciphering Adult Friendships

Defined by qualities of voluntariness, reciprocity, and longevity, adult friendships offer a unique blend of companionship, support, security, loyalty, self-validation, and intimacy. These dimensions underscore the rich tapestry of adult friendships and their pivotal role in enhancing life satisfaction.

PERMA Model: A Framework for Wellbeing

Wellbeing encompasses both hedonic (pleasure and satisfaction) and eudaimonic (meaning and personal growth) elements, with the PERMA model providing a comprehensive framework. This model identifies Positive Emotions, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, and Accomplishments as fundamental pillars, offering insights into how friendships enrich our lives.

Empirical Insights into Friendship and Wellbeing

A synthesis of the research reveals:

  • - A positive correlation between the quality and quantity of adult friendships and overall wellbeing, with specific emphasis on the profound benefits of writing gratitude letters.
  • - Approximately 8% of US adults experience depression annually, with friendships playing a key role in mitigating depressive symptoms.
  • - Individuals with a robust network of friends report significantly higher levels of life satisfaction and positive emotions compared to those with fewer connections.

Identifying Research Gaps

Despite these findings, the literature reveals gaps, including a dearth of studies on certain aspects of wellbeing and a predominant focus on younger adults, which limits the applicability of conclusions across different life stages.

Practical Applications and Future Directions

The evidence suggests that fostering quality adult friendships can be a valuable intervention strategy across various contexts, from educational settings to counseling. This highlights the need for initiatives aimed at nurturing these relationships to bolster psychological wellbeing.

Citation:

Pezirkianidis, C., Galanaki, E., Raftopoulou, G., Moraitou, D., & Stalikas, A. (2023). Adult friendship and wellbeing: A systematic review with practical implications. Frontiers in psychology 14, 1059057. https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2023.1059057
